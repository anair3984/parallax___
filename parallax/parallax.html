Madrid

La Historia

History

Madrid is a city in the center of Spain and also the capital. Madrid was officially classified as the capital in 1561 by Phillip II. Madrid is known for its lively culture, food, clothing, colors, and tradition which we will be exploring further in this site.

La Comida

The Food

Madrid has many popular food items with many diverse ingredients. They have the Cocido Madrileno, it is a pork stew it has chickpeas, meats,vegetables and pork. The typical way to eat cocido madrileño is in two or three courses. Once the chickpeas, meats, and vegetables are finished cooking, cooks separate the broth and use it to make soup with small vermicelli noodles. This soup becomes the first course.The next dish is Hueves Rotos This typical Madrid dish is a plate of potatoes which are freshly fried in Spanish olive oil and tossed with sea salt. The potatoes are topped with perfect over-easy eggs. Depending on where you go, you’ll either break the yolks with the edge of a crusty piece of bread or your server will break them, often at the table.

La Cultura 

The Culture

Madrid is transformation from a muddy town to a booming capital, as well as one of Europe's most vibrant art scenes, all set against a backdrop of unending fiestas. Madrid is worth a second look because of its cultural achievements and the kindness of the residents.Spaniards are known around the world for their love of festivals and celebrations. Las Fallas is one of these events, which are known as "Fiestas" in the area. Las Fallas, held in Valencia in March, is undoubtedly Spain's largest festival, with parades, fireworks, dancing, and ceremonial burning. Cadiz's Carnival, which takes place 40 days before Easter, is another popular event. Spain also has many national holidays that are celebrated throughout the country, including the Epiphany on January 6th, Labor Day on May 1st, and Constitution Day on December 8th, which commemorates the ratification of the country's constitution.Spain has a thriving music and dance culture, with a diverse range of genres spanning from traditional classical music to contemporary music. The country's cultural diversity has a big influence on music and dance, with different regions having distinctive music styles. Flamenco music and dance, which incorporates the classic seguidilla form, are popular throughout Andalusia. Sephardic and copla are two other popular genres in the region. Carlos Cano, Javier Ruibal, Joaquin Sabina, and Luis Delgado are some of Andalusia's most well-known musicians.
